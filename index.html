Why most resumes get rejected before a human even reads them

The ATS Problem — and the Competition Problem

Most companies — including 99% of Fortune 500 companies — use an Applicant Tracking System (ATS): software that automatically screens, ranks, and filters resumes before any human sees them.

If your resume isn't formatted and worded the right way, the system rejects it automatically. It doesn't matter how qualified you are. The robot never passes you through.

And even if you do get through? A single job post on OLJ can receive 300+ applications. The hiring manager isn't reading every one — they're scanning for the ones that immediately stand out. A weak headline or vague bullets and you're skipped in seconds.

This tool fixes both problems — rewrites your resume to pass ATS and be the one that actually gets read.

General Cover Letter
Any role
Write a professional cover letter for the following job application. My name: [Your Name] Role I am applying for: [Job Title] Company/Client: [Company or Client Name] My resume summary: [Paste your rewritten summary here] Key skills and experience: [Paste 3-4 of your best bullets here] Job description or requirements: [Paste the job post here] Instructions: Write a 3-paragraph cover letter. First paragraph: hook with my most relevant experience. Second paragraph: match my skills to their specific needs. Third paragraph: confident close with a call to action. Keep it under 250 words. Professional but not stiff.
VA Pitch (OLJ / Upwork)
VA roles
Write a short, punchy VA job application message for an online job platform like OLJ or Upwork. My name: [Your Name] Role: [VA / Executive VA / Admin VA / etc.] Years of experience: [X years] Top 3 skills: [Skill 1], [Skill 2], [Skill 3] Tools I use: [e.g. GHL, Google Sheets, Trello, Slack] Job post I am applying to: [Paste the job post here] Instructions: Write a 150-200 word application message. Start with a strong first line that immediately shows I understand their problem. Mention my most relevant experience. End with a clear call to action. Do not start with "I am writing to apply." Sound confident and human, not robotic.
Cold Outreach / DM
Direct clients
Write a short cold outreach message I can send via Facebook, LinkedIn, or email to a potential client or employer. My name: [Your Name] What I do: [e.g. VA specializing in operations and automation] Who I am reaching out to: [e.g. a digital agency owner, a coach, an e-commerce brand] What problem I solve for them: [e.g. I help them save 10+ hours a week by automating their workflows] Any mutual connection or reason for reaching out: [Optional] Instructions: Write a 3-5 sentence cold message. Make it feel personal, not copy-paste. Lead with their problem, not my background. End with a low-pressure CTA like asking if they are open to a quick chat. Keep it under 100 words.
